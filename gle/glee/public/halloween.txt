Halloween: History and Celebrations

Halloween, celebrated on October 31st, has ancient roots in Celtic festivals and has evolved into a modern celebration.

Historical Origins:
The festival originated from the Celtic celebration of Samhain, marking the end of harvest season. People believed the boundary between the living and dead was thinnest on this night.

Modern Traditions:

Trick-or-Treating:
Children dress in costumes and go door-to-door collecting candy. This tradition became popular in the 1950s in America.

Costumes:
People dress as ghosts, witches, superheroes, or creative characters. Costume parties are popular among all ages.

Jack-o'-Lanterns:
Carved pumpkins with candles inside originated from an Irish legend about "Stinky Jack."
